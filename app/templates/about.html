About DF Audio Forensics

Digital Forensics Project

DF Audio Forensics is an advanced tool designed to detect audio forgery using sophisticated background noise analysis techniques. Created as part of a Digital Forensics academic project, this application implements several state-of-the-art methods for detecting manipulated audio recordings.

Our Technology

Our audio forgery detection system employs multiple analysis techniques:

  • Background Noise Analysis: Examines the consistency of background noise throughout a recording
  • ENF (Electrical Network Frequency) Analysis: Detects inconsistencies in power line hum (50/60 Hz)
  • Spectral Discontinuity Detection: Identifies abrupt changes in the audio spectrum that may indicate splicing
  • Noise Level Consistency Analysis: Evaluates variations in background noise levels that could reveal editing

Applications

Audio forgery detection has important applications in:

  • Legal evidence verification
  • Media authentication
  • Combating disinformation
  • Security and fraud prevention

Methodology

Waveform Analysis

Analyzes the amplitude patterns of the audio signal over time to detect inconsistencies that may indicate tampering.

Spectrogram Analysis

Examines the frequency content of the audio over time to identify unnatural patterns or discontinuities.

Background Noise Extraction

Isolates the background noise component of the audio for detailed forensic examination.

ENF Analysis

Detects inconsistencies in the electrical network frequency components that should be consistent in authentic recordings.
